# Synthetic V1 Brief: Advanced Packaging and OSAT Selection

Sources used for inspiration: BCG semiconductor supply-chain resilience, McKinsey semiconductor scale constraints, Bain private equity due diligence framing.

## Project Background

A client evaluating growth in advanced packaging wants to understand how customers choose OSAT partners under pressure for yield, speed, and geopolitical diversification. The project needs insight into where premium pricing is sustainable, what technical capabilities are true differentiators, and which execution risks undermine expected margin expansion.

This brief should recruit experts who can screen for direct experience in ATP partner selection, ramp management, and cross-region packaging strategies.

## Target Expert Profile

- Packaging engineering leaders from fabless semiconductor companies.
- Program managers from OSATs with advanced packaging line ownership.
- Procurement leads responsible for packaging partner contracting.
- Quality/reliability specialists involved in ramp qualification.
- Independent advisors with direct ATP project experience.

## Screening Questions (5)

1. In advanced packaging partner selection, which factors typically dominate final decisions: technical capability, cycle time, capacity access, or commercial terms?
2. What are the most common causes of yield instability during early ramp phases, and how are responsibilities allocated contractually?
3. How much pricing premium can top-tier OSATs sustain today for differentiated advanced packaging services?
4. Which customer requirements are driving packaging work to diversify across regions, and where is concentration still accepted?
5. What operational indicators do sophisticated buyers track to evaluate OSAT execution risk before awarding larger volumes?

## Notes for Expert Network Coordinator

- Prioritize experts with hands-on advanced packaging execution since 2023.
- Include both customer and provider-side experts to avoid one-sided bias.
- Emphasize practical implementation knowledge over high-level market commentary.
