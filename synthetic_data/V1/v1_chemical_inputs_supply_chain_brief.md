# Synthetic V1 Brief: Semiconductor Chemical Inputs Supply Chain

Sources used for inspiration: McKinsey chemical semiconductor supply chain in America, McKinsey semiconductor barriers to scale, BCG supply-chain resilience.

## Project Background

A specialty-chemicals supplier supporting semiconductor customers is building a growth plan tied to domestic capacity expansion. The project needs to evaluate demand durability, qualification hurdles, and the practical timeline for shifting from import-reliant inputs to local supply options.

The brief should recruit experts who can assess technical qualification risk, procurement behavior, and where local production provides real commercial advantage vs. symbolic positioning.

## Target Expert Profile

- Semiconductor process-chemistry leaders at fabs/IDMs.
- Procurement heads for wet chemicals and specialty gases.
- Quality and compliance leaders involved in chemical qualification.
- Operations executives from chemical suppliers serving semiconductor accounts.
- Policy-linked industrial program advisors with implementation exposure.

## Screening Questions (5)

1. Which chemical categories are most difficult to localize due to qualification complexity or supplier concentration?
2. What evidence is typically required before fabs shift meaningful volume from incumbent imported sources to new local suppliers?
3. Where do cost premiums for local supply remain acceptable, and where do buyers quickly revert to lower-cost global options?
4. What failure modes most often delay local supplier onboarding in semiconductor chemical programs?
5. Which near-term demand signals best distinguish durable domestic-growth opportunities from temporary policy-driven demand spikes?

## Notes for Expert Network Coordinator

- Seek experts with direct fab qualification involvement.
- Include at least one view from each side: buyer, supplier, and quality/compliance.
- Screen out experts lacking specific semiconductor end-market exposure.
