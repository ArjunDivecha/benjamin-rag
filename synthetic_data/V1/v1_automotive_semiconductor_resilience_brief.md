# Synthetic V1 Brief: Automotive Semiconductor Resilience

Sources used for inspiration: BCG semiconductor supply-chain resilience, BCG cost-resilience challenge, McKinsey semiconductor opportunity/barriers.

## Project Background

An automotive electronics supplier is reassessing its semiconductor sourcing strategy after repeated allocation shocks and volatile lead times. The project aims to determine which resilience levers materially reduce disruption risk without creating unsustainable cost load, and which supplier collaboration models actually improve continuity during demand swings.

The expert network brief should identify stakeholders who can speak to practical trade-offs among dual sourcing, inventory buffers, regional manufacturing, and long-term allocation agreements.

## Target Expert Profile

- Semiconductor sourcing heads at automotive Tier-1 suppliers.
- Supply-chain planners from OEMs responsible for vehicle electronics continuity.
- Commercial leaders from MCU, power semiconductor, and sensor suppliers.
- Operations executives at EMS/manufacturing partners supporting automotive programs.
- Risk and resilience program leaders with crisis response experience.

## Screening Questions (5)

1. Which resilience tactics delivered the strongest service-level improvement in your automotive programs, relative to their incremental cost?
2. Under current market conditions, where is dual sourcing feasible in practice, and where is it still constrained by qualification complexity?
3. How have OEM and Tier-1 contract structures changed to address semiconductor allocation risk since the last major shortage cycle?
4. What inventory policies have proven effective for critical electronic components without creating major obsolescence write-offs?
5. Which supplier behaviors most reliably predict allocation support during tight markets?

## Notes for Expert Network Coordinator

- Balance viewpoints across OEM, Tier-1, and supplier layers.
- Prioritize experts involved in both shortage and normalization periods.
- Exclude experts limited to purely transactional distributor roles without planning visibility.
