# Synthetic V1 Brief: AI Infrastructure Component Demand

Sources used for inspiration: Bain Global Technology Report (AI growth), McKinsey semiconductor opportunity/barriers, BCG cost-resilience supply chain.

## Project Background

A client supplying power-management and connectivity components to AI data-center builds wants to refine its demand planning and customer targeting strategy. The project focuses on whether current hyperscaler ordering behavior reflects durable demand or short-cycle pre-buying, and which component categories are most exposed to design changes or qualification resets.

The objective is to recruit experts who can separate structural AI infrastructure growth from temporary channel distortions and provide practical screening evidence on procurement behavior.

## Target Expert Profile

- Data-center infrastructure procurement leads from hyperscalers or top colocation providers.
- Component sourcing managers at GPU server OEMs.
- Power and thermal architecture specialists with recent deployment experience.
- Distribution/channel executives handling constrained component allocation.
- Semiconductor sales operations leaders with forecast ownership.

## Screening Questions (5)

1. Which AI server component categories currently show the highest mismatch between booked demand and true deployment demand?
2. What triggers a major customer to shift from spot buys to longer-term capacity reservations in this market?
3. How often are power and cooling constraints forcing redesigns that alter component mix within existing programs?
4. What signals best indicate whether current ordering patterns are inventory build vs. end-demand backed?
5. In negotiations today, where do buyers push hardest: pricing, lead time guarantees, or engineering support commitments?

## Notes for Expert Network Coordinator

- Emphasize experts with direct visibility into 2024-2026 planning cycles.
- Include at least one perspective each from hyperscaler demand planning, OEM operations, and component distribution.
- Prioritize experts comfortable discussing process and patterns without disclosing customer-specific confidential forecasts.
