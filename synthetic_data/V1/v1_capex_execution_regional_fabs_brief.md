# Synthetic V1 Brief: Regional Fab Capex Execution and Vendor Readiness

Sources used for inspiration: McKinsey accelerating tech-focused capital projects, BCG supply-chain resilience, Bain due diligence orientation.

## Project Background

A client supporting regional fab build-outs wants to understand why announced timelines diverge from actual ramp outcomes and how vendor ecosystems can improve delivery certainty. The project focuses on schedule-risk root causes, contracting discipline, and execution governance in multi-party capital programs.

The resulting expert network brief should identify stakeholders able to provide specific screening evidence on execution bottlenecks and mitigation practices.

## Target Expert Profile

- Program directors for semiconductor fab construction/ramp initiatives.
- EPC and owner-representative leaders with semiconductor project portfolios.
- Procurement managers for long-lead equipment and critical utilities.
- Commissioning and startup leaders from recently completed fab sites.
- PMO experts responsible for cross-vendor schedule integration.

## Screening Questions (5)

1. What three factors most frequently create schedule slippage between fab shell completion and stable production ramp?
2. Which contract terms are most effective at reducing rework and interface risk across EPC, equipment, and commissioning parties?
3. Where do long-lead procurement assumptions most often prove wrong in current fab programs?
4. Which governance mechanisms (e.g., integrated control towers, milestone reviews) have measurably improved delivery reliability?
5. What early warning indicators best predict whether a fab project is likely to miss initial production targets?

## Notes for Expert Network Coordinator

- Prioritize experts with direct responsibility for delivery outcomes, not only advisory roles.
- Include both owner-side and vendor-side execution views.
- Focus on experts with recent involvement in North America, Europe, or Asia fab expansions.
