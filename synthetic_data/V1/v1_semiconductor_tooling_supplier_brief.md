# Synthetic V1 Brief: Semiconductor Tooling Supplier Strategy

Sources used for inspiration: McKinsey semiconductor scale barriers, McKinsey tech-focused capex delivery, BCG semiconductor supply-chain resilience, Bain Global Technology Report.

## Project Background

A client in semiconductor tooling is evaluating where to focus account coverage and product investment as foundry and memory customers rebalance capex across regions. The project needs clarity on demand timing, the practical cost of adding resilience, and how procurement teams are changing supplier expectations for uptime, service levels, and localization.

The brief should help an expert network identify interviewees who can speak to node-transition bottlenecks, equipment qualification cycles, and commercial negotiations under customer concentration pressure.

## Target Expert Profile

- Former foundry fab operations leaders (process integration, equipment engineering, fab planning).
- Procurement and supplier-quality managers from top foundries and IDMs.
- Regional service leaders from major tool OEMs and high-growth challengers.
- Site launch leaders involved in greenfield fab ramp-up schedules.
- Industry consultants with direct project work on equipment localization programs.

## Screening Questions (5)

1. In the past 24 months, what were the most common reasons a fab delayed equipment qualification after purchase order issuance?
2. How are leading foundries weighting total cost of ownership vs. geographic service footprint when selecting tool vendors?
3. Which spare-part categories create the largest downtime risk today, and how have buyers changed contract language to mitigate that risk?
4. When customers request localized support or local-content commitments, what commercial concessions are vendors usually asked to provide?
5. Where do you see the largest gap between announced fab-capacity plans and realistic equipment installation timelines?

## Notes for Expert Network Coordinator

- Prioritize experts with hands-on involvement after 2022.
- Mix incumbents and challenger-vendor perspectives.
- Exclude experts restricted by active confidentiality obligations on unreleased process nodes.
