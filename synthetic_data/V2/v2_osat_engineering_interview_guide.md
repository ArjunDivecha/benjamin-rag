# Synthetic V2 Interview Guide: OSAT Engineering Director

Sources used for inspiration: BCG semiconductor supply-chain resilience, McKinsey semiconductor scaling constraints.

## Interview Context

This interview is designed to understand advanced packaging execution realities, including yield stabilization, partner selection, and regional delivery considerations.

## Stakeholder Type

Engineering or program leadership at an OSAT or advanced packaging operation.

## Questions

### Opening Questions

1. How has demand mix shifted across packaging technologies over the last two planning cycles?
2. Which parts of your process are currently the most capacity-constrained?

### Core Questions

3. What are the leading causes of ramp delays when onboarding new advanced packaging programs?
4. How do customers typically trade off cycle-time speed versus yield targets in early production phases?
5. Which customer requirements most influence site-selection or regional allocation decisions for packaging work?
6. What internal capabilities (engineering depth, process control, supplier integration) matter most for sustained premium positioning?

### Sensitive Questions

7. In the market, where are customers increasingly pushing for risk-sharing on ramp outcomes?
8. What patterns have you observed in customer switching behavior when incumbent packaging partners underperform?

### Closing Questions

9. What operational metrics are most useful to benchmark OSAT execution quality across peers?
10. Which adjacent roles (customer quality, procurement, reliability teams) would provide the strongest counterpoints to your perspective?
