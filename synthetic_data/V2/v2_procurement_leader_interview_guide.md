# Synthetic V2 Interview Guide: Semiconductor Procurement Leader

Sources used for inspiration: McKinsey semiconductor barriers to scale, BCG cost-resilience challenge, Bain due diligence framing.

## Interview Context

The interview aims to understand how semiconductor procurement organizations are balancing cost, resilience, and supplier concentration in current contracting cycles.

## Stakeholder Type

Procurement or sourcing leader with direct category ownership in semiconductor equipment/components/materials.

## Questions

### Opening Questions

1. How has your sourcing strategy changed since the last major supply disruption cycle?
2. Which categories are currently most difficult to secure at acceptable risk-adjusted cost?

### Core Questions

3. What criteria now determine preferred-supplier status beyond pure unit price?
4. Where is dual sourcing practical today, and where is it still constrained by qualification burden?
5. How do you evaluate the cost of resilience (inventory, localization, second-source onboarding) in annual planning?
6. Which supplier transparency practices most improve your confidence in continuity planning?

### Sensitive Questions

7. In competitive bidding, where do negotiations now concentrate most intensely: price, lead-time commitments, or performance remedies?
8. Without naming suppliers, what contracting clauses have proven least enforceable during stressed demand conditions?

### Closing Questions

9. What signals do you monitor to decide when to shift from tactical buying to multi-year partnership commitments?
10. Which counterpart roles (operations, quality, finance) should we interview to validate procurement-side conclusions?
