# Synthetic V2 Interview Guide: Data Center Infrastructure Architect

Sources used for inspiration: Bain Global Technology Report (AI demand), McKinsey semiconductor scaling constraints, BCG cost-resilience challenge.

## Interview Context

This interview is intended to understand how AI infrastructure build-outs are changing component requirements, procurement priorities, and deployment constraints.

## Stakeholder Type

Data-center architecture or infrastructure planning leader with direct responsibility for AI/accelerated compute deployments.

## Questions

### Opening Questions

1. How have AI workloads changed your infrastructure design priorities over the last 12 months?
2. Which constraints most frequently determine deployment pace today?

### Core Questions

3. What component categories are now gating deployment timelines most often?
4. How do you assess trade-offs among performance, energy efficiency, and supply assurance when selecting platform designs?
5. Where do you see the biggest mismatch between vendor roadmap claims and field deployment realities?
6. What demand-planning assumptions are most fragile in current AI infrastructure investment plans?

### Sensitive Questions

7. In the market, where are buyers increasingly willing to pay premiums for supply or technical support certainty?
8. Without naming partners, what patterns signal elevated execution risk in a supplier relationship?

### Closing Questions

9. What leading indicators should investors or suppliers watch to detect AI demand normalization vs. continued acceleration?
10. Which stakeholder profiles should we interview next to triangulate your perspective (procurement, operations, finance)?
