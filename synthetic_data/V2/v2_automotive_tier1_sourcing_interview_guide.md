# Synthetic V2 Interview Guide: Automotive Tier-1 Electronics Sourcing

Sources used for inspiration: BCG cost-resilience supply chain, BCG semiconductor resilience, McKinsey semiconductor barriers.

## Interview Context

The interview seeks to quantify how automotive electronics sourcing teams are redesigning semiconductor continuity strategies while preserving cost competitiveness.

## Stakeholder Type

Automotive Tier-1 sourcing executive responsible for semiconductor and electronics component categories.

## Questions

### Opening Questions

1. What continuity risks are top of mind in your semiconductor supply base today?
2. How have customer delivery expectations influenced your inventory and sourcing policies?

### Core Questions

3. Which resilience initiatives have delivered measurable service-level improvement, and at what cost?
4. Where does qualification complexity still limit multi-source strategies in critical components?
5. How are OEMs and Tier-1 suppliers currently allocating responsibility for disruption risk in contracts?
6. Which supplier collaboration practices most improve allocation outcomes when markets tighten?

### Sensitive Questions

7. In recent negotiations, what risk protections are you able to secure without materially eroding margin?
8. Which common resilience actions look strong in strategy decks but fail in operational practice?

### Closing Questions

9. If you could change one upstream behavior to reduce shortage risk, what would it be?
10. Which internal stakeholders should we interview to validate your perspective (planning, finance, engineering)?
