# Synthetic V2 Interview Guide: Private Equity Operating Partner (Tech/Manufacturing)

Sources used for inspiration: Bain private equity due diligence, BCG due diligence capability, McKinsey capex execution and supply-chain context.

## Interview Context

The interview is designed to capture investor-side perspectives on commercial diligence, operational risk, and value-creation priorities for tech/manufacturing assets with semiconductor-linked exposure.

## Stakeholder Type

Private equity operating partner, portfolio transformation lead, or transaction advisor with repeated diligence and post-close execution experience.

## Questions

### Opening Questions

1. In the current environment, what factors most often determine whether you advance or exit a tech/manufacturing diligence process?
2. How has your diligence scope expanded compared with pre-2022 transactions?

### Core Questions

3. Which commercial diligence questions most reliably predict downside risk in supplier-concentrated businesses?
4. How do you evaluate management credibility on demand forecasting and capex execution claims?
5. What operational KPIs best indicate whether a portfolio company can absorb supply-chain volatility without margin collapse?
6. Which 100-day initiatives create the fastest risk reduction in semiconductor-adjacent assets?

### Sensitive Questions

7. In competitive deals, where do buyers most frequently underwrite too aggressively today?
8. What recurring issues are discovered post-close that were visible but underweighted during diligence?

### Closing Questions

9. If you had to simplify diligence to three non-negotiables for this sector, what would they be?
10. Which additional stakeholders should we interview to pressure-test investor conclusions (customers, suppliers, program managers)?
