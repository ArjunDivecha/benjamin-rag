# Synthetic V2 Interview Guide: Foundry Operations Leader

Sources used for inspiration: McKinsey semiconductor opportunity/barriers, McKinsey capex execution, BCG supply-chain resilience.

## Interview Context

This interview explores operational constraints and decision dynamics in advanced foundry environments to inform market-entry and account-prioritization strategy for a tooling/services provider.

## Stakeholder Type

Current or former foundry operations executive (fab operations, process integration, or equipment engineering leadership).

## Questions

### Opening Questions

1. How would you describe the top operational priorities for your fab network over the last 12-18 months?
2. Which production metrics receive the most executive attention when utilization becomes volatile?

### Core Questions

3. Where do equipment qualification timelines most commonly extend beyond plan, and why?
4. How do you evaluate trade-offs between best-in-class tool performance and vendor support responsiveness?
5. Which factors drive your decisions on localizing service capability or spare-part buffers?
6. In node-transition periods, what supplier behaviors are most predictive of smooth ramp execution?

### Sensitive Questions

7. In the market, what commercial terms are becoming more common when large customers seek stronger uptime guarantees?
8. Without naming firms, where do you see recurring gaps between supplier commitments and actual delivery performance?

### Closing Questions

9. If you were advising a supplier entering this space today, what two mistakes would you tell them to avoid?
10. Which adjacent stakeholders should we speak with next to pressure-test these views?
