Executive Summary (1/5)
10
•The global data collection and annotation for AI market is estimated at USD ~5.5 billion in 2022, projected to grow at an average of 30+% per year over the next 5 years, to reach USD ~22 billion by 2027•Computer vision (video, image, sensor fusion), which is language-agnostic, represents the majority of the market by data type, while language-dependent annotation (text, audio) represents over a third of the market. However, text is projected to grow faster than other data types – largely thanks to the advent of Large Language Models (LLMs)•By geography, the US and Canada represent ~60% of the global market, followed by Europe (~15%), China (~10%) and Asia-Pacific ex China (~10%)•By offering category, Computer Vision annotation (excluding Relevance) and Relevance & Discovery (e.g. search / ad / product / map relevance) and represent the majority of the market (~35% and ~30%, respectively), although Conversational AI (~10%) and LLM Training (~7.5%) are growing fast•Within language-dependent annotation, non-English annotation represents a minority of the overall market (~30%). However, clients typically prefer having one vendor cover both English and non-English annotation, especially if the workflow is the same – making multi-lingual capabilities an important differentiator•Most customers are expected to continue outsourcing the majority of their AI data annotation – although some (e.g. [ORG], LLM builders and AI startups) may in-source part of their spend due to concerns over privacy/security, data quality and/or cost•With the rise of generative AI and increased automation in data labeling, market demand is shifting towards higher quality, more complex work that requires more qualified / specialized annotators (e.g. prompt engineering / response writing, domain knowledge in law / finance / medicine / coding / etc.)

Executive Summary (2/5)

•Large companies (defined as companies with over USD 2 billion in annual revenue and/or part of the Global 2000 list) account for ~70% of the global external data collection and annotation spend•[ORG] companies (Google, [ORG], [ORG], [ORG], [ORG]) are by far the largest segment (~45% of the market), each spending hundreds of millions annually on data services•The second biggest segment is non-tech enterprises (~18% of the market) – split across multiple verticals, with the largest being retail, financial services and healthcare. Spend is usually concentrated in the top 3-5 players per industry, who each spend millions (or tens of millions) annually•Large automotive and autonomous driving companies (Toyota, Tesla, GM Cruise, Honda, etc.) account for ~16% of the market, with companies typically spending tens of millions annually (mostly on computer vision projects)•Large tech companies (defined as tech companies with annual revenue over USD 2 billion, excluding [ORG]) are another significant segment (~11% of the market), with companies each spending in the millions (or tens of millions) annually•AI startups typically have limited and volatile budgets; they are best served in a “self-serve” way as a bet on their future•Outside of [ORG], most clients prefer AI data service providers to use their own labeling platforms (rather than clients’ tools); this represents an opportunity for [ORG] to improve margins through increased automation and control over delivery•Customers’ purchasing process vary based on company size/sophistication, use case and volume purchased, but for enterprise customers it usually involves seven main stages. A key stage, which [ORG] does not seem to perform well in due to lack of brand awareness / industry reputation, is vendor pre-selection – during which, once needs are defined, customers decide which vendors to consider. This is typically not a sophisticated process, and is influenced by existing vendor relationships, vendor reputation for the target use case, recommendations from colleagues and industry peers, and/or a cursory online search. This pre-selection is typically driven by the technology and/or product team in charge of deploying the model.
