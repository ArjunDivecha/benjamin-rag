Executive Summary (1/4)
8
• CX/EX feedback collection solutions targeting [ORG] firms in the US currently represent a USD ~90 million market, which is
expected to reach USD ~140 million by 2029, driven by greater penetration and increased spending
• By solution: CX/EX platforms represent the largest segment (~65%), due to higher average spend and higher penetration in
Enterprise. Self-service survey tools account for ~20% of the market due to lower average spend, despite high penetration
• By vertical: Construction and Engineering account for ~90% of the market – Engineering (~50%) due to higher adoption and
Construction (~40%) due to large number of firms. Architecturemarket is relatively small, due to lower number of firms
• By customer size: Enterprise represents~55% of the market, due to higher adoption and average spend. Mid-market at ~25%
• Market growth will be driven by positive [ORG] industry outlook (which increases CX/EX budget available), competition for clients,
labor shortages, and gradual technology transformationin the [ORG] industry
• Some uncertainty remains around [ORG] industry outlook – due to potential tariffs, fewer migrant workers exacerbating talent
shortage, and uncertainty around interest rates. However, this uncertainty can also create opportunities CX/EX solution
providers, as revenue dips and competition for talent are drivers of adoption
• In the next 5 years, ~60% of market growth in CX/EX platforms will come from greenfield and brownfield opportunities, while
~40% will derive from spend increase of existing CX/EX platform users
• ~100 Mid-market firms and ~50 Enterprises are expected to adopt a CX/EX platform from scratch (greenfield opportunity),
while ~60 Mid-market firms and ~30 Enterprises are expected to upgrade from existing tools (self-service / free / CRM add-
on) to a CX/EX platform (brownfieldopportunity)

Executive Summary (2/4)
9
• Complexityof CX/EX feedback collection needs vary by customer size and CX/EX maturity
• Often, [ORG] firms needs are relatively straightforward(e.g. post-project customer feedback), and can be met by tools with self-
service capabilities
• Robust platforms like [ORG] become more relevant as needs mature, and/or when clients value a more hand-held
approach
• Negative events, such as revenue dips or employee churn, are major triggers for initiating CX/EX programs; upgrading solutions
can be more growth-motivated, once customerssee the value of CX/EX programs
• Preference for more robust CX/EX platforms is driven by 3 main factors: sophistication of the marketing leader, company
technologicalmaturity overall, and company size / complexity / budget
• Buyers discover solutions through two main channels: word-of-mouth / industry peers, and online search
• Solutions are evaluated on having just the right fit in terms of functionality for the buyer’s level of CX/EX maturity, ease of use
and price – though this varies by customer segment
• Functionality,integrationwith existing systems/processesand ease of use overtakeprice for larger and more mature buyers
• Switching costs are relatively high, especially for larger buyers, due to integrations(e.g. with CRM system), importance of ease of
use (vs. learning about a new platform), and the relatively small investmentwhich CX/EX solutions represent for large firms
• Buyers serving federal customers are less likely to use survey tools beyond CPARS, making them a lower GTM priority for [ORG]

• [ORG]’s sweet spot are customers who are relatively early in their CX/EX stage, and value [ORG]’s “hand-held”
approach. Targeting potential customers early in their purchasing journey, before they start considering other solutions, will
maximize [ORG]’s chances success
• As such, the following measures would enhance [ORG]’s growth:
• Continuing to build a strong brand awareness in the [ORG] industry to be top-of-mind for customers evaluating CX/EX feedback
collection tools (through presence at industry conferences,podcast interviews, organizing industry-specific events, etc.)
• Developing an entry-level, competitively-priced solution for customers with basic needs: this will allow [ORG] to get a
foothold in new accounts early, before they become accustomedto other platforms (and face high switching costs)
• Tailoringits value proposition depending on clients’ needs profiles, in addition to emphasizing its [ORG] industry experience:
• For clients with complex needs: point out [ORG]’s competitive Total Cost of Ownership (including customization) vs.
service-assisted implementations of [ORG]/[ORG], and high level of dedicated support and guidance (including for
mid-sized companies)
• For clients with low-complexity needs: highlight ease of use (vs. [ORG]) and flexibility and adaptability of solutions, with
an upgrade path to more advanced capabilities compared to [ORG]
• For clients who perceive CX/EX as high priority: underline agile, dedicated service team for fast deployment, in addition to
with high-touch, comprehensivesupport with strategicadvisory
• For clients who perceive CX/EX as lower priority: emphasize fast time-to-value and rapid ROI from hand-held onboarding

• To detect potential customers, leading indicators include recent adoption of CRM tools (e.g. [ORG], [ORG], [ORG]),

Potential [ORG] Market Headwinds
3 Market Analysis – Potential Headwinds
Sources: Oxford Economics, Pew Research Center, US Census Bureau data, White House, BM Interviews, Research & Analysis
• Executive orders imposing 25% tariffs on all US imports of steel and aluminum with no exceptions or exclusions, due to take effect on March
12, and a general 25% tariff on all Canadian and Mexican imports, delayed for 30 days until March 6
• Tariffs likely to drive up material costs of steel, aluminum, timber and other building materials in the short term; many companies unable to
shelter themselves through hedging or contractual arrangements may account for price hikes
• Potential disruptions from delivery delays of materials due to increased customs inspections and supply chain turbulence
• 26% of US construction workers are immigrants and 13% of workers are unauthorized (per Pew Research Center), the largest share of any
sector; foreign-born workers, regardless of status, fill ~30% of trade jobs like carpentry, plastering, masonry and electrical roles
• Deportations and migrant worker visa restrictions could exacerbate the labor supply issue that the sector already faces, slowing down
project timelines, increasing labor costs and an inducing an overall construction industry slowdown
• A series of executive orders already expand “expedited removals” and restrict employment-based visas; US Immigration and Customs
Enforcement have ramped up efforts to arrest and detain people unauthorized to be in the US
• Public projects in limbo as federal agencies ordered to stop/pause disbursing Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) and Inflation
Reduction Act (IRA) funding, clean energy tax credits within the IRA and spending associated with the CHIPS act
• New administration is expected to bring a lower tax environment with fewer regulations; however, uncertainty over public policy may delay
investment decisions
