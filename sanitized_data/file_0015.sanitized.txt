Executive Summary (2/5)
11
•Customers’ purchasing process vary based on company size/sophistication, use case and volume purchased, but for enterprise customers it usually involves seven main stages, including:•Pre-selection: once needs are defined, customers decide which vendors to consider. This is typically not a sophisticated process, and is influenced by existing vendor relationships, vendor reputation for the target use case, recommendations from colleagues and industry peers, and/or a cursory online search. This pre-selection is typically driven by the technology and/or product team in charge of deploying the model.•Vendor recommendation: once a shortlist is established, customers reach out to pre-selected vendors to submit bids and/or conduct pilots; customers often evaluate several vendors simultaneously and may end up choosing more than one vendor (to give them flexibility / a backup option). For larger companies, legal and/or purchasing are involved once the technology/product team indicates their vendor preference(s).•Once vendors meet table stakes (i.e. supporting a specific use case / data type, and meeting compliance / security requirements for enterprise buyers), customers’ top 5 Key Purchasing Criteria (KPC) are data quality/accuracy, scalability (volume and/or speed), variety of data, price and vendor reputation. We are continuing to refine and validate this list•Price can rank higher, depending on customer size, volume of data purchased, project criticality and task complexity / vendor competition
