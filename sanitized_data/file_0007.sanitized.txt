Executive Summary (1/2)
10
• [ORG] feedback collection solutions targeting [ORG] in the US currently represent a USD ~90 million market, which is
expected to reach USD ~141 million by 2029, driven by increased penetration and spend increases
• Adoption in [ORG] is lower than in [ORG] verticals, but market consensus is that it will grow
• By customer size: Enterprise represents~60% of the market, due to higher adoption and average spend. Mid-market at ~25%
• By vertical: Construction and Engineering account for ~90% of the market – Engineering (~50%) due to higher adoption and
Construction (~40%) due to large number of firms. Architecturemarket is relatively small, due to lower number of firms
• By solution: [ORG] platforms represent the largest segment, due to higher average spend and higher penetration in Enterprise.
Self-service survey tools account for ~20% of the market due to lower average spend, despite high penetration
• Preference for more robust [ORG] platforms is driven by 3 main factors: sophistication of the marketing leader, company
technologicalmaturity overall, and company size / complexity / budget
Market 
Sizing
2 Executive Summary PRELIMINARY
• [ORG] feedback collection needs for [ORG] are often relatively straightforward(e.g. post-survey customer feedback), and can
be met with simple tools like [ORG]
• More robust platforms like [ORG] become more relevant as needs mature
• Buyers discover solutions through two main channels: word-of-mouth / industry conferences, and online search
• Solutions are evaluated on having just the right fit in terms of functionality for the buyer’s level of [ORG] maturity, ease of use
and price – though this varies by customer segment
• Functionality,integrationwith existing systems/processesand ease of use overtakeprice for larger and more mature buyers
• Switching costs are relatively high, especially for larger buyers, due to integrations(e.g. with CRM system), importance of ease of
use (vs. learning about a new platform), and the relatively small investmentwhich [ORG] solutions represent for large firms
• Buyers serving federal customers are less likely to use survey tools beyond [ORG], making them a lower GTM priority for [ORG]
